Atlanta Bankruptcy FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about filing bankruptcy in Atlanta and the Northern District of Georgia.

General Questions

How do I file bankruptcy in Atlanta?

Complete a pre-filing credit counseling course (online, $15-25). Then file your petition at the N.D. Ga. courthouse at 75 Ted Turner Dr SW, Atlanta, GA 30303. Filing fee: $338 for Chapter 7 or $313 for Chapter 13. Most people hire an attorney.

Should I file Chapter 7 or Chapter 13?

Chapter 7 is faster (3-4 months) and best if you qualify via the means test. Chapter 13 is better for saving a home from foreclosure but has a 72.9% dismissal rate in N.D. Ga. Compare chapters.

Can I file without an attorney?

Yes, but it is risky. Mistakes can cause dismissal or loss of property. Contact Atlanta Legal Aid for free help.

How much does it cost?

Chapter 7: $338 filing + $1,200-$2,500 attorney. Chapter 13: $313 filing + $3,500-$5,000 attorney (usually through plan). Full cost breakdown.

The Automatic Stay

Will bankruptcy stop wage garnishment?

Yes. The automatic stay immediately stops wage garnishment upon filing.

Will bankruptcy stop foreclosure?

Yes. The automatic stay stops foreclosure immediately. Georgia allows non-judicial foreclosure in 60 days, so acting fast is critical. Keep your house guide.

Will bankruptcy stop repossession?

Yes. The stay prevents repo. If your car was recently repossessed, quick filing may get it back. Keep your car guide.

Will it stop lawsuits and collections?

Yes. Most civil suits and all collection activity are paused. Creditors who violate the stay face contempt.

Property and Exemptions

Will I lose my house?

If equity is under $21,500 ($43,000 joint), you keep it. Chapter 13 lets you catch up on payments. Exemptions guide.

Will I lose my car?

Georgia's $5,000 vehicle exemption protects most cars. Wildcard covers more. Full car guide.

Is my retirement protected?

Yes. 401(k), IRA, and pension accounts are fully exempt in Georgia.

Can I use federal exemptions?

No. Georgia has opted out. State exemptions only.

After Bankruptcy

How long on my credit?

Chapter 7: 10 years. Chapter 13: 7 years. Most people improve within 1-2 years. Rebuilding credit guide.

Can I file again?

Yes, with waiting periods. Ch. 7 to Ch. 7: 8 years. Ch. 13 to Ch. 13: 2 years. Full guide.

What happens at the 341 meeting?

A trustee asks questions under oath for 5-10 minutes. Held ~30 days after filing. Creditors rarely attend. Full 341 meeting guide.
