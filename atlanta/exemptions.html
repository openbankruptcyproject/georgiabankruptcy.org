Georgia Bankruptcy Exemptions for Atlanta Filers

Key Exemptions Explained

Homestead Exemption -- $21,500

Georgia's homestead exemption protects up to $21,500 of equity in your primary residence. For married couples filing jointly, this doubles to $43,000. "Equity" means your home's market value minus what you owe on the mortgage.

Example: Your home is worth $250,000 and you owe $240,000 on the mortgage. Your equity is $10,000 -- fully protected by the $21,500 exemption.

If your equity exceeds the exemption in a Chapter 7 case, the trustee could potentially sell your home and pay you the exemption amount from the proceeds. In Chapter 13, you keep your home but may need to pay more to unsecured creditors.

Vehicle Exemption -- $5,000

You can protect up to $5,000 of equity in one motor vehicle. If you owe more than the car is worth, you have no equity and the car is fully protected.

Example: Your car is worth $12,000 and you owe $9,000. Your equity is $3,000 -- fully protected. But if the car is worth $12,000 and you own it free and clear, you have $12,000 in equity and only $5,000 is protected.

The wildcard exemption can cover additional vehicle equity. Full guide to keeping your car →

Wildcard Exemption -- $600 + $5,000

Georgia's wildcard exemption lets you protect $600 of any property. Additionally, if your homestead exemption is not fully used, you can apply up to $5,000 of the unused portion to any property.

If you rent (no homestead used): You get $600 + $5,000 = $5,600 wildcard to apply to anything -- cash, a car, tools, electronics, etc.

If you own a home and use the full homestead: You only get the base $600 wildcard.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use federal exemptions in Georgia?

No. Georgia has opted out of the federal bankruptcy exemptions. You must use Georgia state exemptions. This means you cannot choose between state and federal -- Georgia's exemption amounts are the only option available.

What if my home equity exceeds $21,500?

In Chapter 7, the trustee could sell your home and give you the $21,500 exemption amount from the proceeds. In Chapter 13, you keep your home but must pay unsecured creditors at least as much as they would have received in Chapter 7 (the "best interest of creditors" test).

Is my retirement account protected?

Yes. 401(k), 403(b), IRA, and pension accounts are fully exempt in Georgia bankruptcy. There is no dollar limit. These funds cannot be taken by the trustee or creditors.
