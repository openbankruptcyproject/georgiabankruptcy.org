Chapter 7 Bankruptcy in Atlanta, Georgia

Chapter 7 is the fastest path to debt relief. Most Atlanta filers receive a discharge in 3-4 months, eliminating credit card debt, medical bills, and other unsecured obligations.

What Is Chapter 7?

Chapter 7 bankruptcy -- sometimes called "liquidation bankruptcy" or a "fresh start" -- eliminates most unsecured debts. A court-appointed trustee reviews your assets, and any non-exempt property can be sold to pay creditors. In practice, most Chapter 7 cases in Atlanta are "no-asset" cases, meaning filers keep everything they own.

Atlanta residents file Chapter 7 at the Northern District of Georgia courthouse at 75 Ted Turner Dr SW, Atlanta, GA 30303.

The Means Test in Georgia

To file Chapter 7, you must pass the means test. This compares your household income to Georgia's median income:

Household SizeGeorgia Median Income (approx.)
1 person$55,000
2 people$70,000
3 people$78,000
4 people$91,000

If your income is below the median, you automatically qualify for Chapter 7. If your income is above the median, you may still qualify after deducting allowed expenses for housing, transportation, taxes, and other necessities.

What Debts Does Chapter 7 Eliminate?

Debts that ARE eliminated:

  • Credit card balances
  • Medical bills and hospital debt
  • Personal loans and payday loans
  • Utility arrears (gas, electric, water)
  • Lease obligations and broken contracts
  • Deficiency balances after repossession or foreclosure

Debts that are NOT eliminated:

  • Student loans (except in rare hardship cases)
  • Recent income taxes (generally last 3 years)
  • Child support and alimony
  • Court fines and restitution
  • Debts from fraud or willful injury
  • DUI-related obligations

Georgia Exemptions in Chapter 7

Georgia does not allow filers to choose federal exemptions. You must use state exemptions:

Chapter 7 Timeline in Atlanta

  1. Pre-filing credit counseling -- Required within 180 days before filing. Takes about 1 hour online. Cost: $15-25.
  2. File petition -- Submit forms to the N.D. Ga. courthouse. The automatic stay takes effect immediately.
  3. 341 Meeting of Creditors -- Approximately 30 days after filing. You answer questions under oath from the trustee. Usually lasts 5-10 minutes. What to expect →
  4. Objection period -- Creditors have 60 days after the 341 meeting to object to discharge of specific debts.
  5. Debtor education course -- Must complete before discharge. Takes about 2 hours online. Cost: $10-25.
  6. Discharge -- Typically 60-90 days after the 341 meeting. Total time: approximately 3-4 months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I qualify for Chapter 7 in Atlanta?

You must pass the means test. If your household income is below Georgia's median (approximately $55,000 for a single filer), you automatically qualify. If above, you may still qualify after deducting allowed expenses. Means test details.

What debts does Chapter 7 eliminate in Georgia?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts including credit cards, medical bills, personal loans, and utility arrears. It does not eliminate student loans (except in rare hardship cases), recent taxes, child support, or alimony.

How long does Chapter 7 take in Atlanta?

Most Chapter 7 cases in N.D. Ga. take 3-4 months from filing to discharge. The 341 meeting occurs about 30 days after filing, and discharge follows approximately 60 days later.

Will I lose my house in Chapter 7?

If your home equity is under Georgia's $21,500 homestead exemption ($43,000 for married couples), you can keep your home. If equity exceeds the exemption, the trustee could sell it. Keeping your house guide.
