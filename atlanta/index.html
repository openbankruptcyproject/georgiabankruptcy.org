The Northern District covers metro Atlanta including Fulton, DeKalb, Gwinnett, Cobb, Clayton, and surrounding counties. It handles the vast majority of Georgia's bankruptcy filings.

The 72.9% Problem: Atlanta's Chapter 13 Dismissal Rate

The Northern District of Georgia has a 72.9% Chapter 13 dismissal rate, making it one of the worst-performing districts among major metros in the entire country. Nearly 3 out of 4 Chapter 13 cases filed in Atlanta fail to reach discharge.

Additionally, 44.8% of filers in N.D. Ga. have filed bankruptcy before -- a prior filer rate that suggests many debtors are being cycled through the system repeatedly without achieving lasting relief.

What this means for you:

  • Choose your attorney carefully. High dismissal rates are often driven by high-volume practices that file cases without adequate preparation.
  • Understand the commitment. Chapter 13 requires 3-5 years of monthly payments. If your budget cannot sustain this, Chapter 7 may be a better option.
  • Ask about dismissal rates. A responsible attorney should be willing to discuss their firm's completion rate.
  • Consider Chapter 7 first. If you qualify, Chapter 7 is faster (3-4 months) and has a much higher success rate.

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 in Atlanta

Chapter 7 -- Fresh Start

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts in about 3-4 months. You must pass a means test based on Georgia's median income.

  • Discharge in 3-4 months
  • Eliminates credit cards, medical debt
  • Must pass the means test
  • Filing fee: $338

Chapter 13 -- Payment Plan

Chapter 13 lets you keep property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Good for saving a home from foreclosure.

  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • Keep your home and car
  • 72.9% dismissal rate in N.D. Ga.
  • Filing fee: $313

Non-Judicial Foreclosure in Georgia

Georgia is a non-judicial foreclosure state, which means your lender can foreclose on your home without going to court. The process can move quickly -- as fast as 60 days from the first missed payment to foreclosure sale.

Filing bankruptcy triggers the automatic stay, which immediately halts foreclosure proceedings. If you are facing foreclosure in Atlanta, time is critical.

  • Chapter 13 allows you to catch up on missed mortgage payments over 3-5 years while keeping your home
  • Chapter 7 can delay foreclosure but will not save your home long-term if you cannot make payments
  • The automatic stay stops the foreclosure sale the moment your case is filed

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does bankruptcy cost in Atlanta?

Chapter 7 filing fees are $338 and Chapter 13 filing fees are $313. Attorney fees in Atlanta typically range from $1,200 to $2,500 for Chapter 7 and $3,500 to $5,000 for Chapter 13. Two required credit counseling courses cost $25-$50 total. Full cost breakdown.

Where do I file bankruptcy in Atlanta?

Atlanta residents file in the Northern District of Georgia at the U.S. Bankruptcy Court, 75 Ted Turner Dr SW, Atlanta, GA 30303. Phone: (404) 215-1000. Website: ganb.uscourts.gov.

Why is Atlanta's Chapter 13 dismissal rate so high?

N.D. Ga. has a 72.9% Chapter 13 dismissal rate. High dismissal rates can indicate systemic issues including aggressive filing practices, insufficient pre-filing screening, or inadequate debtor education. Nearly 3 out of 4 Chapter 13 cases fail. Learn more about dismissal.

Can I keep my car if I file bankruptcy in Atlanta?

Georgia's vehicle exemption protects up to $5,000 of equity. If your equity is under $5,000, you can typically keep your car. The wildcard exemption ($600 + up to $5,000 of unused homestead) can protect additional equity. Full car guide.

How fast can Georgia foreclose on my home?

Georgia is a non-judicial foreclosure state. Foreclosure can happen in as few as 60 days. Filing bankruptcy triggers the automatic stay, which immediately stops the foreclosure process.
