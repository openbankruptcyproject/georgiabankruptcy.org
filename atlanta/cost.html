How Much Does Bankruptcy Cost in Atlanta?

A complete breakdown of every cost you will face when filing bankruptcy in Atlanta, from court fees to attorney fees to credit counseling.

Attorney Fees in Atlanta

ChapterTypical RangeNotes
Chapter 7$1,200 -- $2,500Usually paid in full before filing
Chapter 13$3,500 -- $5,000Often paid through the repayment plan

Attorney fees in Atlanta tend to be slightly higher than the state average due to the cost of living in the metro area.

Important: Given N.D. Ga.'s 72.9% Chapter 13 dismissal rate, be cautious of firms that advertise very low fees or "no money down" Chapter 13 filing. Low upfront cost can indicate a high-volume practice that may not provide adequate attention to your case.

Credit Counseling Costs

  • Pre-filing credit counseling: $15-25 (required within 180 days before filing)
  • Post-filing debtor education: $10-25 (required before discharge)

Both courses can be completed online in 1-2 hours each.

Fee Waivers and Payment Options

Chapter 7 fee waiver: If your household income is below 150% of the federal poverty guidelines, you can apply to have the $338 filing fee waived entirely.

Installment payments: Both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 filers can request to pay the filing fee in up to 4 installments over 120 days.

Chapter 13 attorney fees: Most attorneys allow fees to be paid through the repayment plan, reducing upfront cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Chapter 7 cost in Atlanta?

Chapter 7 filing fee is $338. Attorney fees typically range from $1,200 to $2,500. Credit counseling adds $25-$50. Total: approximately $1,563 to $2,888.

How much does Chapter 13 cost in Atlanta?

Chapter 13 filing fee is $313. Attorney fees range from $3,500 to $5,000, but are usually paid through the repayment plan. Upfront cost can be as low as $338-$363.

Can I get a fee waiver?

Chapter 7 filers below 150% of the poverty line can apply for a full fee waiver. Chapter 13 filers can pay the filing fee through their plan.
